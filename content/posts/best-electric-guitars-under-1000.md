## The $1,000 Sweet Spot

Under $1,000 is where the magic happens in electric guitars. You're past the beginner tier where corners are cut, but you haven't entered the diminishing-returns zone of $2,000+ instruments. The guitars in this range are gigging-quality instruments that professionals use on real stages.

The difference between a $300 guitar and a $700 guitar is massive. The difference between a $700 guitar and a $2,000 guitar? Much smaller than you'd think.

## #3: PRS SE Custom 24 - Most Versatile

**Price:** ~$600 | [Check price on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=PRS+SE+Custom+24&tag=musicgrear20-20) | [Sweetwater](https://www.sweetwater.com/store/search?s=prs+se+custom+24) | [Guitar Center](https://www.guitarcenter.com/search?typeAheadSuggestion=&Ntt=prs+se+custom+24) | [zZounds](https://www.zzounds.com/search?q=prs+se+custom+24)

PRS (Paul Reed Smith) built their reputation by bridging the gap between Fender and Gibson. The SE Custom 24 delivers that promise at $600 - humbuckers that can be split to single-coils via push/pull, a comfortable double-cutaway body, and a tremolo that stays in tune.

### What You Get
- Mahogany body with maple top
- Maple neck, Wide Thin profile
- 24 frets
- 2x 85/15 "S" humbuckers with coil-split
- PRS tremolo bridge
- Made in Indonesia

### Why It's Here
The coil-split is the game-changer. Pull up on the tone knob and the humbuckers split to single-coils, giving you Strat-like tones from a humbucker guitar. You effectively get 4 guitars in one: bridge humbucker, neck humbucker, bridge single, neck single.

24 frets gives you access to the full two-octave range that Fender's 22-fret designs can't reach. For lead players, this matters.

### Pros
- Coil-split gives you humbucker AND single-coil tones
- 24 frets for full range
- Beautiful flame maple top (looks like a $2,000 guitar)
- Tremolo stays in tune well
- $600 is excellent value
- PRS fit and finish is consistently high

### Cons
- PRS tremolo not as smooth as Fender's 2-point system
- Some players find the Wide Thin neck too flat
- Coil-split tones are good but not identical to real single-coils
- Less aftermarket pickup options than Fender/Gibson

## FAQ

**Q: Should I buy new or used?**
A: Used guitars are excellent value. A used Fender Player Stratocaster ($500-600) gives you 90% of the Player II experience. Check [Reverb](https://reverb.com/marketplace?query=fender+player+stratocaster) for the best used deals.

**Q: Do I need to set up a new guitar?**
A: Most guitars benefit from a professional setup ($50-75). It adjusts the neck relief, action, intonation, and pickup height for your playing style. It's the single best investment you can make after buying the guitar.

**Q: Which pickup configuration is best?**
A: SSS (3 single-coils) for clean versatility. HH (2 humbuckers) for rock power. HSS or HSH for the best of both worlds. The PRS SE Custom 24's coil-split HH is the most versatile single configuration.

**Q: Can a $450 guitar gig professionally?**
A: Absolutely. The Epiphone Les Paul Standard and Squier Classic Vibe are used on professional stages worldwide. A $450 guitar with a $75 setup plays beautifully.
