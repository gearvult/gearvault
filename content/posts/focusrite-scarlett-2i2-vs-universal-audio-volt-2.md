## Quick Verdict

The **Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 4th Gen** (~$180) wins on reliability, driver stability, and features (auto-gain, Air mode). The **Universal Audio Volt 2** (~$220) wins on sound character with its vintage preamp mode and built-in 76 compressor. Both are excellent. ### What Sets It Apart

**Vintage Preamp Mode** is a real analog circuit (not a plugin) that adds harmonic saturation to your input signal. It's the same type of coloration that makes vintage Neve and UA preamps so sought after. Flip the switch, and vocals, guitars, and drums gain warmth and body that the Scarlett simply can't replicate.

**76 Compressor** is a hardware compressor circuit on the input path. On the Volt 2, it's available on Channel 1. It smooths out vocal dynamics during recording - loud parts get tamed, quiet parts stay present. This is the sound of countless hit records, built into a $220 interface.

**Build Quality** - the Volt 2 has a metal chassis with a retro-industrial aesthetic. It looks and feels more expensive than the plastic Scarlett. The large central volume knob is satisfying to use.

### Pros

- Built-in 76 compressor (hardware, not software)
- Premium metal build quality
- Looks gorgeous on a desk

- Bundled with UA Spark plugin collection

### Cons

## Sound Comparison

### Clean Recording (No Processing)
With all effects off, both interfaces record clean, professional audio. The differences are subtle:
- **Scarlett 2i2:** Transparent, neutral, what-you-sing-is-what-you-get
- **Volt 2:** Very slightly warmer in the low-mids, even with vintage mode off

### With Features Engaged
This is where they diverge significantly:
- **Scarlett 2i2 with Air Mode:** Adds a 2-3dB high-frequency shelf around 8-12kHz. Vocals sound more "present" and "open." Great for dull-sounding rooms or darker microphones.
- **Volt 2 with Vintage + 76:** Adds harmonic richness, gentle compression, and a "finished" quality to vocals. You could skip post-processing entirely and publish the raw recording.

### The Honest Truth
If you A/B tested both interfaces with all processing off, most people couldn't tell the difference. The magic is in the character features: Air mode vs Vintage/76. That's what you're really choosing between.

## FAQ

**Q: Will either of these work with the Shure SM7B?**
A: The Scarlett 2i2 4th Gen has +69dB of gain, which is borderline sufficient. The Volt 2 at +55dB will likely need a Cloudlifter. For SM7B use, the Scarlett is the better choice of the two.

**Q: Do I need 192kHz recording?**
A: No. Record at 44.1kHz or 48kHz. Higher sample rates consume more storage and CPU with no audible benefit for 99% of users.

**Q: Can I use these with GarageBand / Logic / Ableton / FL Studio?**
A: Yes, both work with every major DAW on Mac and Windows.

**Q: What about the Scarlett Solo for saving money?**
A: The Solo (~$130) has one mic input and one instrument input. If you'll only ever record one source at a time, it's great. The 2i2 lets you record two sources simultaneously (useful for stereo recording or recording guitar + vocals at once).
