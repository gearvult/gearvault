## Why Studio Monitors Matter

Your headphones lie to you. Consumer headphones boost bass and treble to make everything sound exciting. Studio monitors tell you the truth about your mix.

If your mix sounds good on flat, accurate monitors, it'll sound good everywhere - in the car, on earbuds, on Bluetooth speakers, on club systems. That's the point. Monitors are the most important investment in any home studio after your ears.

## Buying Guide

### Your Room Matters More Than Your Monitors

The biggest thing holding back home studio mixes isn't the monitors - it's the room. A $260 pair of JBL 305Ps in a treated room will produce better mixes than $2,000 Genelecs in an untreated bedroom.

Before upgrading monitors, consider:
- **Acoustic panels** on first reflection points ($100-200)
- **Bass traps** in corners ($100-200)
- **Monitor isolation pads** to decouple from your desk ($30-50)

### Size Guide
- **3.5" (iLoud MTM):** Desks under 3 feet wide, bedrooms under 100 sq ft
- **5" (all others):** Typical home studio, bedroom/spare room
- **8" (not in this list):** Dedicated studio rooms 150+ sq ft

### Placement Tips
- Position monitors at ear height, forming an equilateral triangle with your head
- Pull them at least 8 inches from walls
- Angle them inward 30 degrees
- Use isolation pads under them (not directly on desk)

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a subwoofer?**
A: For most home studios under $500 total: no. The KRK Rokit 5 G4 and Adam T5V both reach into the 40s Hz range, which is sufficient for most mixing. Add a sub later if you produce bass-heavy music.

**Q: Can I use these for casual listening too?**
A: Absolutely. Studio monitors sound great for music listening - just different from consumer speakers. They're more detailed and honest.

**Q: Yamaha HS5 or HS7?**
A: The HS7 has a 6.5" woofer and reaches lower (43Hz vs 54Hz). If your room is larger than 120 sq ft and you make bass-heavy music, the HS7 (~$500/pair) is worth the upgrade. Otherwise, the HS5 + a sub later is a better path.

**Q: Why not just mix on headphones?**
A: Headphones put sound directly in your ears, bypassing room acoustics. This means your stereo image and bass levels will be wrong. Mix on monitors, check on headphones.
