## What You Need to Know About PA Systems

A PA (Public Address) system amplifies sound for an audience. At its simplest, you need:

1. **Powered speakers** (amp built in - just plug in and go)
2. **A mixer** (to control multiple inputs - mics, instruments, music playback)
3. **Microphones and cables**

At the $2,000 budget, you can get a professional-quality powered speaker pair that handles venues up to 200-300 people without breaking a sweat. Add a mixer for $200-400 and you have a complete system.

**Powered vs Passive:** At this budget, always go powered (active). Powered speakers have the amplifier built in, which means perfect amp-to-speaker matching, less gear to carry, and simpler setup. Passive systems require separate amplifiers and are more complex to configure.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a subwoofer?**
A: For speech, acoustic music, and small venues: no. For DJs, bands with bass-heavy music, or venues over 150 people: yes. The QSC CP12 + KS112 combo is the best sub-$2,000 option.

**Q: How many people can these cover?**
A: The QSC K12.2 and Yamaha DXR12 comfortably cover 200-300 people indoors. The JBL EON715's 15" drivers can push even further. The EV ZLX-12BT is best for up to 150.

**Q: Should I buy 12" or 15" speakers?**
A: 12" is the sweet spot for most uses - better midrange clarity, easier to transport. 15" goes louder and deeper but is larger and heavier. For bands and speech: 12". For DJs and large venues: 15".

**Q: Can I use these as stage monitors?**
A: Yes. All of these have a wedge-shaped cabinet that angles correctly when placed on the floor as a monitor. The QSC K12.2 and Yamaha DXR12 have dedicated monitor presets.

**Q: What mixer should I get?**
A: The Yamaha MG10XU (~$230) is the standard for small systems. For more channels, the MG16XU (~$400). Both have USB for recording directly to a laptop.
