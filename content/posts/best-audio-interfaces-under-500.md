## What to Look For in an Audio Interface

Before we dive into our picks, here's what actually matters when choosing an interface under $500:

**Preamp Quality** - This is the single biggest factor affecting your recordings. A great preamp captures detail, warmth, and dynamic range that no amount of post-processing can add later.

**Conversion Quality (AD/DA)** - The analog-to-digital and digital-to-analog converters determine how faithfully your recordings translate and how good your playback sounds. At this price range, most modern interfaces have excellent converters.

**I/O Count** - How many inputs and outputs do you need? Solo vocalist/guitarist: 2 inputs is fine. Recording a band or running hardware synths: you'll want 4+.

**Latency** - Low latency means you can monitor yourself in real-time while recording without annoying delay. Look for interfaces with direct monitoring or very low round-trip latency.

**Driver Stability** - The best-sounding interface is useless if the drivers crash. Focusrite and MOTU have the best track record here.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need an audio interface if I'm just making beats?**
A: If you're producing entirely in-the-box (software only), an interface mainly improves your monitoring quality. The MOTU M4's DAC will make your headphone mixes translate better. But if you're on a tight budget, you can start without one.

**Q: USB-C or USB-A?**
A: Most modern interfaces are USB-C but include a USB-A adapter. Performance is identical either way.

**Q: Will these work with my iPad/iPhone?**
A: The MOTU M4 is class-compliant and works with iOS out of the box. Focusrite Scarlett works with iOS via their control app. Check compatibility for others.

**Q: Do I need the more expensive Focusrite Scarlett models?**
A: The preamp quality is identical across the 4th Gen range. You're only paying for more inputs/outputs. If 2 inputs is enough, the Scarlett 2i2 ($180) is the same sound quality as the 4i4.

**Q: What about the Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 vs Universal Audio Volt 2?**
A: The Scarlett 2i2 has better driver stability and auto-gain. The Volt 2 has warmer preamps and the 76 compressor. For vocals: Volt 2. For everything else: Scarlett 2i2.
